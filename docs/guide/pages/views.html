Views

[%= project %] views exist to interact with the DOM. A view has an el by which all DOM manipulation, creation, and observation is conducted. Views are equipped with an emitter which is a simple pubsub interface. It's existence is vital to the usability of views in [%= project %]. Without it, you end up with random callbacks, assumptions about child implementations, and overall horrible architecture.

[%= project %] comes equipped with a few different types of views:

  • pie.view - a simple DOM wrapper enabling management of events, observation of models, etc.
  • pie.activeView - a pie.view with templating functionality. More on templating later.
  • pie.formView - a pie.activeView designed to standardize and enhance the behavior of forms.

pie.view

pie.view is the most rudimentary implementation of DOM management in [%= project %]. It's purpose is to provide a way to easily and efficiently query, observe, and update DOM.
